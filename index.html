US Demographic Health Factors

Relation Between Demographics and Health Risks

Lower income was consistently associated with higher smoking, also charts shows smoking rate is higher among adults between 36-42 years old.

Overall obesity decreased with increased levels of income, as we see the states with higher median income and lower poverty percentage have significantly lesser obesity prevalence. Among adults between 36-38 years old, the obesity is higher.

Charts shows overall healthcare improves with increase in median income, in general the states with median income above $65,000 have better healthcare, with few exceptions

The data is collected from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System has been used to explore the relations between demographics and health risk factors. Each marker represents an individual state in the USA.
